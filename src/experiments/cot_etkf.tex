As a final validation for the model, the trained neural network inside the ensemble Kalman filter is tested.

Evaluating the neural network produced linear warping operator in the ensemble Kalman filter method presents a more intricate task.
Due to the computationally involved ETKF algorithm, a quick computation of the error metrics for a larger dataset as for the determistic model is not possible.
As the chosen focus of this work is in the filtering distribution estimation and completing of partial observations, it is also impossible to know what the true state would be to which compare results.
Also due to the separate problems of ensemble generation reflecting a realistic uncertainty for pure prediction problems as discussed earlier, and validation metrics related to probabilstic ensemble forecasts, the forecasting problem is given less emphasis.

As with evaluations of the deterministic model, the same COT images of $128 \times 128$ pixels and similar synthetic examples are used.
Despite using images of the same shape as the neural network works with, it is not a hard limitation of the proposed framework.
Larger images could be downsampled for the neural network to work with larger state vectors in the ETKF or vice versa.

For fine tuning the ETKF model integrating the deterministic neural network presented in Subsection~\ref{subsec:etkfmodel}, a few assumptions were made related to the chosen objectives of the verfications with the COT data and synthetic examples.
The ETKF parameters were chosen with the aim of giving a small uncertainty for the observations with the intent of returning the observation values where available, and otherwise values advected from the precious state.
This choise stems from the quickly evolving COT data not only within advective fashion and thus outside the capability of the chosen modelling and also to better verify the results.
In possible operational use of the framework these parameters are naturally subject to case by case tuning depending on the actual data.
For best results, a similar scaling as when training the network of $[0, 1]$ range of the data was used also for the ETKF\@.
As the perturbations for the ensemble according to the strategy presented in Subsection~\ref{subsubsec:etkftuning} result in slightly different data range for the state vectors, the neural network scales the data before generating updated warping operators.
For the adding spatially varying noise in generation of the initial ensemble and inflation, noise is generated for the entire image area but multiplied by the image values pointwise before adding to the images.
Localisation is performed in $16 \times 16$ pixel spatial blocks to better maintain spatial structures in the data.
An ensemble size of 500 is used as it appears to give a good balance of high quality results and adequate computing time.

While Kalman filtering has the benefit of offering uncertainty estimates, a major difficulty in these kinds of tasks is that the magnitude of uncertainty is effectively controlled by the filter parameters.
Especially with the ensemble filters where the state propagator model uncertainty is largely defined by the used inflation technique, many unknown parameters must be guessed or estimated by hand.
While accurate inflation methods would be crucial in order to get accurate uncertainty estimates, without much data to do verification with, the important parameters must often be tuned with just the goal of obtaining visually pleasing results.
In wider use, however, the model uncertainty could be better estimated from previous model errors to get more accurate information.

For possible forecast usage, the inflation and initial ensemble generation are even more important as no observations are available to support the forecasts.
In addition, as the computation a forecast distribution in the ensemble filter is not limited by the standard Kalman filter assumptions, the range of possibilities for ensemble generation and inflation is much wider.
In a recent related work related to the forecasting problem presented in~\cite{intraday} an ensemble is generated by multiplying the fourier transforms of a noise field and a state estimate.
In that work an advective model is also combined with an autoregressive model that could potentially also improve the predictive performance of the neural network of this work with regards to the COT data.
